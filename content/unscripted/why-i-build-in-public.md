There's a quiet war happening in IoT security. On one side, you have billion-dollar companies shipping devices with the security posture of a wet paper bag. On the other, you have a handful of researchers trying to make things right — mostly for free, mostly in their spare time.

I build in public because **closed-source security is an oxymoron**. You can't secure what you can't inspect.

Every tool I release — RFSploit, BlueSploit, HARDAX — they're not just tools. They're statements. They say: "This attack exists. This defense is possible. Here's the code. Verify it yourself."

The IoT underground doesn't need more NDAs. It needs more GitHub repos.

> "In a world of closed devices, open-source is rebellion."

That's not just a tagline. It's a mission.
