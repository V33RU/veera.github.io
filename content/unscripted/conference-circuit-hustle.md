People see the stage. They see the slides. They don't see the 3 AM soldering sessions the night before, the demo that worked perfectly at home but crashes on the conference WiFi, or the 14-hour bus ride to a city you've never been to.

Running a security village at c0c0n was one of the hardest things I've done. You're responsible for:

- Hardware that attendees will inevitably try to break (that's the point)
- A schedule that never goes as planned
- Making sure nobody accidentally bricks the shared equipment

But when you see someone's face light up the first time they dump firmware from a real device? That's it. That's the whole reason.

The hacker conference scene in India is growing fast. BSides chapters are popping up everywhere. The energy is real.

If you're thinking about submitting a CFP - do it. The community needs your voice more than you think.
